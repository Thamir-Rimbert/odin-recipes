Spaghetti Squash

Back to Home

Ingredients

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 2 Tbs Olive oil
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Brush the cut sides of the squash with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the squash halves cut side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Roast in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and easily pierced with a fork.
  6. While the squash is roasting, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a small skillet over medium heat.
  7. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  8. Once the squash is done roasting, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  9. Use a fork to scrape the flesh of the squash, creating spaghetti-like strands.
  10. Transfer the spaghetti squash strands to a large bowl.
  11. Add the sautéed garlic, chopped parsley, and chopped basil to the bowl with the squash strands. Toss to combine.
  12. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste.
  13. Serve the spaghetti squash warm, garnished with extra fresh herbs if desired. Enjoy!
