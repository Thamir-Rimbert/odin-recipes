Eggplant & Chickpeas

Back to Home

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbs Avocado oil
  • 1c Yellow onion, peeled and diced
  • 1 Tbs Garlic, chopped
  • 1 Tbs Ginger, minced
  • 1 1/2 Tbs Spice blend

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cut eggplants in half lengthwise. Score the flesh in a crosshatch pattern, being careful not to cut through the skin. Brush the cut sides with 1 tablespoon of avocado oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the eggplants cut side down on the prepared baking sheet. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and golden brown.
  4. While the eggplants are roasting, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of avocado oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for 5-7 minutes, or until softened and translucent.
  5. Add the chopped garlic and minced ginger to the skillet and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent burning.
  6. Add the spice blend to the skillet and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to toast the spices and release their flavors.
  7. Add the canned tomatoes (with their juice) to the skillet. Use a spoon to break up the tomatoes into smaller pieces. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Add the cooked chickpeas to the tomato sauce and stir to combine. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes to heat through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Once the eggplants are roasted, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly. Use a spoon to scoop out some of the flesh, creating a cavity for the chickpea mixture.
  10. Spoon the chickpea mixture into the cavities of the roasted eggplants, dividing it evenly among them.
  11. Serve the stuffed eggplants hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired. Enjoy!
